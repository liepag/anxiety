Keep in mind, people with anxiety disorders experience symptoms differently. You might have an anxiety disorder if you experience at least three of the following symptoms for more than six months.

Thoughts
You think that danger is everywhere
You think that something is dangerous when it’s not
You think that bad things are going to happen
You are very critical of yourself
Your worrying is out of control
Feelings
You feel tense most times
You feel restlessness, irritable or angry
You find it difficult to focus and concentrate
You are hypervigilant to threat cues
Sensations
Your heart is pounding or racing
You feel numb
You sweat a lot
You have an upset stomach
You have headaches
You are very tired all the time
You feel like your throat is closing up
You are short of breath
You feel dizzy
Behaviours
You avoid situations, people and things
You cling to people for safety and you find separation very difficult
You do things over and over again
You seek for reassurance a lot
You rely on others to get you through difficult times or you won’t allow people to help you
You can’t sleep
You prepare for all that could go wrong
